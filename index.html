Sketch Narrative

Context: This sketch lets users set a timed focus session, where a lightbulb is gradually getting brighter, symbolizing increasing focus and productivity over time.

  • Context: This timer is for students or professional wanting a calm and ambient visual timer for focus or work sessions.
  • Design decisions: I chose to use the visual of a lightbulb since it symbolizes focus. As it brightens, it visually represents productivity increasing throughout a session. This makes time progression much more engaging as compared to a mechanical clock. I also decided to have ambient background changes, with the background gradually getting brighter as time passes, reflecting the growth of light from the bulb. This visual background shift helps to maintain a peaceful environment while also marking progress. Lastly, instead of having text input, I decided to include simple preset options for different timers. This approach allows users to simply focus on their work, without having to worry about trying to figure out a complex clock design.
  • Future work: A feature I'd like to implement for the future would be to add a soft dimming phase after a study session to signal break time and a chance to reset. Additionally, I think it would be cool to allow users to choose from various visual themes such as a candle or sunrise to make it seem more customizable according to their mood.

Sketch Narrative

Context: This sketch represents time passing through the flipping of pages in a book, symbolizing steady learning and progress throughout a study or reading session.

  • Context: This is ideal for students or readers who want a visual sense of progression rather than a countdown-based timer.
  • Design decisions: For this sketch, I decided to implement the page flipping animation. Each page flipping marks the passage of time, reflecting how focus sessions build knowledge with each page. The visual's goal is to display time in a gradual manner to show progression. In terms of color palette, I decided to keep it fairly simple, using beige tones for paper and brown for the cover, creating a familiar visual that is appealing to look at. I also decided to have each page flip every minute, giving users a sense of rhythm and progression instead of stress or urgency.
  • Future work: For the future, I think it would be unique to include page numbers as a way to show how much time as passed. This will give users a visual of what time they're currently on. Additionally, I think it would be cool to allow users to customize their chapter size for how long they'd like to work/be productive for.
